Now continue from the Repair Case-first redesign and focus specifically on smooth transition, monetization integration, and preserving the existing TorqueShed foundation without breaking the current app.

Objective:
Integrate monetization areas naturally into the new TorqueShed experience without making the app feel spammy, unfinished, or like a generic marketplace. Monetization should feel like a helpful extension of diagnostics, repair planning, parts sourcing, tool recommendations, expert help, and seller activity.

Transition requirements:
1. Do not remove the existing Garage/Bay concept entirely.
2. Convert Bays into category/community filters that support the new Repair Case system.
3. Preserve existing users, bay memberships, thread data, and visual styling where possible.
4. Any existing thread should be migrated or displayed as a basic Repair Case.
5. Add fallback handling for old posts that do not yet have full diagnostic data.
6. Maintain the current dark mobile-first TorqueShed style with orange accent color.
7. Fix any existing navigation typo such as “Bavs” and ensure labels are clean and professional.
